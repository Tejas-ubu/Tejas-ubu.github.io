02 —

Five archetypes of the AI-aware mind

Beneath the data lies something more revealing — how people construct their moral relationship with technology. We clustered respondents not by demographics, but by the internal logic connecting their answers.

Each archetype represents a coherent worldview: a particular balance of trust, skepticism, responsibility, and agency. These aren't labels — they're thought patterns.

How we built these

Responses across 13 questions were mapped onto 5 ethical dimensions. Clusters emerged from co-occurring answer patterns — respondents whose views aligned across trust, accountability, and concern dimensions.

What "archetype" means

Not a label imposed from outside, but a coherent internal logic. Each archetype has a characteristic way of framing risk, assigning responsibility, and thinking about AI's future.

The 5 dimensions

Trust · Verification Rigor · Corporate Accountability · Environmental Priority · Transparency Demand. Each respondent scores across all five; archetypes are peaks in this space.

Trust
How much do they take AI output at face value? Credulous, skeptical, or deeply mistrustful?
Verification Rigor
How systematically do they cross-check AI claims? Always, sometimes, or never?
Corp. Accountability
Who's responsible for AI harms — the company, the user, or a shared governance model?
Env. Priority
Does the carbon cost of AI weigh on them? Would they accept worse models for a greener footprint?
Transparency Demand
How strongly do they want AI labeled, cited, and explained to the public?
Axis of Concern
Is their primary worry about bias, misinformation, IP theft, deepfakes, or environmental harm?
